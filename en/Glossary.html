Glossary

This glossary explores the symbols and meanings of swords across various works, from mythological legends to futuristic tales. Discover how these iconic weapons embody power, honor, and destiny.

Andúril

The sword reforged from Narsil in The Lord of the Rings. A symbol of royalty and destiny, it represents Aragorn's legitimacy to rule.

Excalibur

The legendary sword of King Arthur in the film Excalibur (1981). It symbolizes divine power and royal legitimacy.

Ice

The Valyrian steel sword of House Stark in Game of Thrones. A symbol of power and honor, used to deliver justice.

Longclaw

Jon Snow's sword, inherited from House Mormont. Forged from Valyrian steel, it embodies lineage and destiny.

Needle

Arya Stark's light sword in Game of Thrones. A symbol of her independence and rejection of traditional expectations.

Quantum Blade

A futuristic sword made of pure energy, featured in science fiction stories. It represents the fusion of tradition and technology.

Lightsaber

The iconic weapon of the Jedi in Star Wars. Although futuristic, it retains the classic symbolism of the sword: power and the struggle between good and evil.

Valyrian Steel

A rare and powerful metal in the Game of Thrones universe, used to forge legendary swords like Ice and Longclaw.

Boromir

In The Lord of the Rings, Boromir wields a sword that reflects his inner struggle between bravery and the temptation of power.

Labored and Lost

A composition by Lorne Balfe in Assassin's Creed Valhalla, accompanying moments of doubt and inner struggles, often associated with the hero Eivor's sword.

Mythological Sword

The sword in mythological tales is often a symbol of destiny, power, and royalty, like Excalibur or Andúril.
